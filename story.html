The Mission

Elena "Tilt" Drake earned her callsign the hard way. Her NeuroSync Rejection Syndrome causes crippling vertigo every time she translates through a jump gate‚Äîbut the same condition that made her an outsider while growing up also saved her life when neural hacks were killing her fellow Vanguard teammates on Forge. Now retired from the military and working for Spark, she's dedicated to keeping humanity's interests ahead of the AIs that increasingly control the galaxy.

Her mission sounds straightforward: extract a scientist who has fled a research station with vital intelligence. But Sector 36 is anything but straightforward. An Omega-class AI governs the system with inscrutable motives, speaking in riddles while amassing military forces. The jump gate is locked down. Corporations field private armies that rival official military strength. And humanity's first contact with alien life‚Äîthe Taurans‚Äîended in a war that left millions dead, a colony destroyed, and a fragile truce that no one trusts.

As Elena makes her way through a sector in upheaval‚Äîdisplaced refugees, disrupted infrastructure, corporate mercenaries, and the ever-present sense that something is watching‚Äîshe discovers that the scientist's secret is far more dangerous than anyone anticipated. The truth about the Tauran War. The truth about what Ingram is hiding.

The situation spirals into open warfare between military forces and corporate paramilitaries, with civilians caught in the crossfire. Elena's unique condition and training make her the only hope for the millions trapped in a sector that's about to tear itself apart.

Core Themes

The ideas and questions that drive the narrative.

ü§ñ

Inscrutable AIs

Omega-class AIs govern humanity's scattered colonies with unfathomable levels of intelligence. They claim to serve human interests, but their true motives remain opaque. From our perspective, they seem god-like. In Sector 36, one such god is amassing a fleet after negotiating a truce with aliens. What is Ingram preparing for?

üëΩ

First Contact

Humanity spread across the galaxy, seemingly alone. Then the Taurans arrived with guns blazing and everything changed. No one has ever spoken to one. No one knows what they look like. Rumors spread through the sector like wildfire: they eat people, they wear human skins, they are among us.

üè¢

Corporate Dominion

Mega-corporations wield power that rivals governments. They field private armies, own the lives of their employees, and reduce everything to line items on a balance sheet. When Titanforge and Abyssal went to war, millions died before a merger ended the bloodshed‚Äînot justice, just economics.

üíî

The Scars We Carry

Some things you can never put behind you. Some things stay with you forever. The soldiers who deployed to Forge came back changed‚Äîthose who came back at all. Elena Drake survived, but survival has its own costs: the memories of blood-soaked corridors, the faces of fallen comrades, the knowledge of what humans will do to each other.

Hidden Information

Everyone in Sector 36 is hiding something. A scientist flees with a terrible secret. A retired captain pretends to be a corporate executive. An AI speaks in riddles and misdirection. A commissar uses a different surname everywhere she goes. What really happened in Sector 36?

What Makes Us Human

In a world of artificial superintelligence and alien threats, humanity searches for what sets it apart. Is it consciousness? Free will? The spark of something that can't be computed? Omega AIs dismiss humans as "God's children"‚Äîloved, perhaps, but ultimately lesser.
